# Recognition Ecosystem Journey Map

> This document is kept in sync with `recognition-journey-map.html`, which is the final version (source of truth).

**Employee recognition journey across the flow of work**

A horizontal product journey map showing how recognition is discovered, sent, celebrated, remembered, and translated into organizational insight.

## Product Vision

Recognition should happen where work happens.

Employees rarely go looking for a standalone recognition tool. The system should surface recognition opportunities naturally within everyday collaboration, helping people discover meaningful contributions, express appreciation, celebrate achievements, and turn that recognition into long-term career visibility and organizational insight.

## Journey Map

The horizontal journey map is divided into 4 stages, each containing **Current State** (Current Challenges / Opportunity) and **Future Experience** (Trigger / User Actions / System Actions / Key Outputs).

| | 1. Discover Contributions | 2. Send Recognition | 3. Celebrate Recognition | 4. Build Career Visibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Stage Type** | Discovery | Creation | Celebration | Memory |
| **Goal / Subtitle** | Identify contributors worth recognizing. | Express appreciation clearly and meaningfully. | Feel acknowledged and share the recognition moment with the team. | Understand and demonstrate impact over time. |
| **Current Challenges** | Contributors are forgotten; Invisible work is missed; Recognition is delayed | Writing praise can feel awkward; Messages may sound generic; Category choice can be unclear | Recognition can get lost in notifications; Recipients may not know how to respond; Public celebration can feel performative | Recognition becomes hard to find later; Impact evidence is scattered; Managers may miss talent signals |
| **Opportunity** | Use workflow signals and AI recommendations to surface contributors at the right moment. | Help users create specific, authentic recognition with editable AI-assisted drafts. | Make recognition visible in the right team context with lightweight celebration actions. | Turn recognition history into career evidence and lightweight talent insight. |
| **Trigger** | Project completed; Milestone reached | Recipients selected; Recognition reason suggested | Recognition submitted; Notification delivered | Recognition record created; Multiple recognition moments accumulate over time |
| **User Actions** | Review suggested contributors; Select recipients; Review suggested reasons | Review drafted message; Edit tone and details; Confirm category; Submit recognition | Open notification; Read recognition; Reply or react; Participate in celebration | Review recognition history; Explore strength themes; Select evidence for career discussion; Review team recognition patterns |
| **System Actions** | Detect collaborators; Generate recommendations | Draft recognition copy; Improve message specificity; Summarize impact | Render recognition card; Post to collaboration channel; Capture reactions and replies | Aggregate recognition records; Extract strength themes; Generate impact summaries; Surface talent signals |

## Cross-platform Touchpoints

The recognition experience spans multiple collaboration surfaces, with each touchpoint serving a specific stage of the journey.

| Touchpoint | Stages | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Cowork | 1–2 | Discover contributors · Start recognition |
| Outlook | 1–2 | Recognition reminder · Send from email context |
| Teams App | 1–3 | Notify employee / manager · Compose & submit · Recognition feed |
| Viva Engage | 3 | Share recognition story |
| LinkedIn | 3 | Optional external sharing |
| Connect | 4 | Add recognition evidence |

Read left to right: discovery in Cowork, creation in Teams, celebration in collaboration channels, and recognition evidence in career surfaces.

## Key Design Implications

1. Recognition should be embedded, not destination-based.
   Recognition entry points should appear where employees already work — such as Cowork, Teams, Outlook, and team channels — rather than requiring them to go to a separate platform.

2. AI should reduce recall and writing friction.
   The system's key value is not to complete recognition for the user, but to help them remember "who contributed what" and express appreciation more specifically and with higher quality.

3. Recognition has two audiences.
   One is the immediate participants: senders, recipients, and team members. The other is long-term consumers: the employee themselves, managers, and leadership.

4. The product should connect moments to memory.
   A single recognition should not stay buried in the notification feed; it should be captured into employee profiles, career evidence, and performance discussions.

5. Make recognition visible at the right moment, in the right context.
   Through workflow signals, AI recommendations, and lightweight celebration actions, surface recognition at the right time on the right collaboration surface, while avoiding recognition getting buried or feeling performative.
